How Handle Life and School

organization time: all semester
sucess rate: 80%

Are you struggling with handling school, homework, a job, and a social life? Here are some tips to handle what life has to offer while you're a journalism student. Or any student for that matter.

  • Tips and Tricks

  • Plan schedule wisely
  • Prioritize school over work
  • Use a planner
  • Leave free time for yourself
  • SLEEP

  1. Plan your work and school schedules wisely.

    When putting together a school schedule try and leave plenty of time for homework, studying, a job, and any other activities. If it is impossible to schedule classes close together due to when classes are offered use the gap time productively. If the gap is only a few hours go to the library and get work done. If there is a larger gap either use this time for homework or work during that gap.

  2. Make sure to prioritize school and homework over a job.

    Always remember that school is more important than a part time job. Don't over schedule work or accept more shifts than you can handle. The job is temporary and school will lead to a career.

  3. Use a planner or a calander to stay organized.

    At the beginning of the semester add every assignment and test to a planner and/or calander on your phone. This might be tedious but it will be helpful throughout the semester.

  4. Leave free time to relax and spend time with friends and family.

    In order to keep stress and anxiety levels down it's important to schedule time to relax. Mental and physical health is important so make sure to eat and rewind.

  5. SLEEP!

    As useful as an all nighter might seem it's not good for your body or your brain. When working and going to school it's important to get a good nights sleep every night so that you can stay focused and think more clearly.
